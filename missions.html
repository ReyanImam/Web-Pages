Apollo 11

First manned mission to land on the Moon. Launched on July 16, 1969, and successfully landed on July 20, 1969.

Voyager 1 & 2

Launched in 1977 to explore the outer planets and now traveling in interstellar space.

Mars Rover Missions

Various missions to explore the surface of Mars, including Curiosity and Perseverance rovers.

Hubble Space Telescope

Launched in 1990, it has provided unprecedented views of the universe.

International Space Station (ISS)

A habitable artificial satellite that serves as a microgravity research laboratory.

New Horizons

Launched in 2006, it conducted a flyby of Pluto in 2015 and is now exploring the Kuiper Belt.

Perseverance Rover

Landed on Mars in February 2021 to search for signs of ancient life.

Mercury-Atlas 6 (Friendship 7)

Launched on February 20, 1962, this was the first American manned orbital flight, piloted by John Glenn.

Apollo 13

Launched on April 11, 1970, it was the third crewed mission intended to land on the Moon, but it suffered an in-flight emergency.

Skylab

America's first space station launched in 1973, it was occupied by three different crews, contributing valuable scientific knowledge.

Chandrayaan-1

Launched in 2008 by India, it was India's first mission to the Moon, discovering water molecules on the lunar surface.

Rosetta

Launched in 2004, Rosetta became the first spacecraft to orbit a comet and land a probe on its surface (Philae on Comet 67P).

Kepler Space Telescope

Launched in 2009, Kepler was designed to discover Earth-like planets orbiting other stars.

InSight

Landed on Mars in November 2018 to study the planet's interior and seismic activity.

Artemis I

Launched in November 2021, this uncrewed mission marked the first flight of the Space Launch System (SLS) and the Orion spacecraft.

SpaceX Crew Dragon

Launched in May 2020, Crew Dragon became the first commercial spacecraft to transport astronauts to the ISS.

James Webb Space Telescope

Launched on December 25, 2021, this telescope is designed to observe the universe in infrared light, providing deeper insights into cosmic phenomena.

OSIRIS-REx

Launched in 2016, this mission collected samples from the asteroid Bennu and is expected to return them to Earth in 2023.

Spirit and Opportunity Rovers

Launched in 20 03, these twin rovers were designed to explore the surface of Mars, providing valuable data about the planet's geology.
